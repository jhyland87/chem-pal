Comparing prices across suppliers is only fair if you're comparing like with like.
ChemPal does two things automatically so you can trust the **Price** and **Qty**
columns side-by-side.

## Every price in your currency

Suppliers list prices in their own currencies — dollars, euros, pounds, yen, and
more. ChemPal detects each price's currency, converts it using up-to-date exchange
rates, and displays everything in **the currency you choose**. The **Price** column
header shows which currency you're viewing.

To pick your currency, open **Settings → Behavior → Currency**. When you select a
non-USD currency, ChemPal shows the current conversion rate right below it (e.g.
**"1 USD = 0.92 EUR"**) so you know the rate being applied.

## Quantities normalized for fair comparison

Suppliers describe amounts inconsistently — "500g", "0.5 kg", "1 lb", "100 mL".
ChemPal normalizes these to a common scale so that sorting by quantity, and
comparing price-per-amount, actually lines up no matter how each vendor wrote it.

Put together, this means you can:

- Sort the **Price** column to find the cheapest option in *your* currency, and
- Compare the **Qty** column across suppliers without doing unit math in your head.

## A note on accuracy

- Exchange rates are fetched live and cached briefly during a session, so a
  converted price is an estimate — the amount you actually pay is set by the
  supplier at checkout in their own currency.
- Prices in ChemPal come straight from the suppliers and can change at any time.
  Always confirm the current price on the supplier's own page before buying.
