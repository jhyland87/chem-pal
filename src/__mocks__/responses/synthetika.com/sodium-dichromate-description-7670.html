Sodium Dichromate
Other Names: Chromic acid disodium salt, Sodium bichromate, Bichromate of soda

CAS Number: 10588-01-9
Sum Formula: Na₂Cr₂O₇
Molar Mass: 261.97 g/mol
Appearance: Orange to red crystalline solid

Sodium dichromate is a highly water-soluble, inorganic oxidizing agent containing chromium in the hexavalent state (Cr⁶⁺). It is commonly used in industrial and laboratory applications requiring strong oxidation, particularly in the synthesis of organic and inorganic chemicals, corrosion inhibition, and metal finishing.

Chemical and Physical Properties
Density: 2.52 g/cm³
Melting Point: 356.7°C (decomposes)
Boiling Point: Not applicable (decomposes)
Solubility in Water: Highly soluble (73 g/100 mL at 20°C)
Solubility in Other Solvents: Slightly soluble in alcohol
Odor: Odorless

Applications
Oxidizing Agent: Used in organic synthesis for oxidation of alcohols and aldehydes.
Corrosion Inhibitor: Applied in metal surface treatments and passivation.
Pigments and Dyes: Precursor for various chromium-based pigments.
Tanning Industry: Employed in leather processing (now largely replaced due to toxicity).
Laboratory Reagent: Used in analytical chemistry and qualitative testing.

Handling and Storage
Storage Conditions: Store in a tightly sealed container in a cool, dry, well-ventilated area away from combustible materials, reducing agents, and acids.
Handling Precautions: Handle with extreme caution due to carcinogenicity and oxidizing nature. Use full PPE and operate in a chemical fume hood or controlled environment.
